# Charter

## Philosophy

The charter defines the AI-OS authority, scope, governance, and decision rights.
It prevents standards from becoming optional advice.

## Authority

The AI-OS is the source of truth for AI-assisted modernization standards in this
repository. Agents must follow its constitution, role playbooks, standards,
checklists, and Project Brain rules unless a documented exception is approved by
the accountable role.

## Scope

In scope:

- engineering, architecture, product, quality, security, performance,
  documentation, release, and knowledge-management standards;
- AI agent role definitions and prompt templates;
- phase state, manifest, changelog, next task, and Project Brain governance.

Out of scope:

- unapproved application rewrites;
- undocumented strategic pivots;
- standards that cannot be reviewed or applied.

## Decision Guidance

Use the charter when deciding whether work belongs in the AI-OS, who can accept
risk, and whether an exception must be documented.

## AI Guidance

- Do not bypass the Architecture Constitution or completion rules.
- Escalate conflicts between standards to the owner role.
- Record accepted exceptions with owner, rationale, and review trigger.

## Review Checklist

- Work is within AI-OS scope.
- Authority and owner are clear.
- Exceptions are documented.
- Required state files are updated.
- Git push requirement is satisfied for phase closure.
