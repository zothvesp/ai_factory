# Retrospective Loop

## Purpose

The Retrospective Loop turns completed work, incidents, review friction, and
delivery surprises into durable improvements.

## Entry Criteria

- A phase, release, incident, or significant review has completed.
- Evidence is available: goals, outputs, defects, risks, metrics, and feedback.
- Participants or role perspectives are known.

## Activities

- Compare outcome against goal, acceptance criteria, and exit criteria.
- Identify what improved delivery safety and what caused friction.
- Capture lessons, recurring risks, debt, glossary terms, and standards gaps.
- Convert improvements into roadmap or next-task entries.
- Close or update risks and debt when repayment happened.

## Outputs

- Lessons learned.
- Updated risks, debt, roadmap, or standards backlog.
- Process improvement actions.
- Next phase recommendation.

## Exit Criteria

- Actionable lessons are recorded.
- Owners and triggers exist for follow-up work.
- Project Brain reflects durable knowledge.

## Checklist

- Evidence is used, not memory alone.
- Lessons are specific and reusable.
- Follow-up has owner and trigger.
- Debt and risks are updated.
- Next task reflects the highest-value improvement.
