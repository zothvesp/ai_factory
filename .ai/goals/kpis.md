# KPIs

## Purpose

KPIs measure ongoing health and progress toward strategic objectives. They guide
decisions but are not goals by themselves.

## Rules

- KPIs must define metric, source, cadence, owner, interpretation, and action.
- Use KPIs to monitor trends, not to replace acceptance criteria.
- Avoid vanity metrics that count activity without outcome.
- Pair KPIs with qualitative review when they can be gamed.

## Entry Format

```markdown
- KPI:
- Purpose:
- Source:
- Cadence:
- Owner:
- Healthy range or target:
- Action when outside range:
```

## Bad Example

```text
KPI: Number of documents generated.
```

## Good Example

```text
KPI: Percentage of completed standards with manifest entry, review checklist,
examples, AI guidance, and no skeletal sections.
```

## AI Guidance

- Do not optimize a KPI at the expense of mission or quality.
- Connect KPIs to product, metrics, or executive standards.
- Update roadmap when KPI trends expose systemic risk.

## Review Checklist

- KPI measures an outcome or meaningful health signal.
- Source and cadence are clear.
- Owner and action threshold exist.
- Gaming risk is considered.
- KPI informs decisions.
